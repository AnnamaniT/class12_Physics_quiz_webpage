Question4: Three charges + 4q , + q and Q are placed on a straight line with equal separation. In order to make the net force on q to be zero, the value of Q will be:

a) +4q

b) +2q

c) -3q

d) -4q

Solution

Force on charge q due to the charge 4q is $F_{1} = \frac{4kq^{2}}{r^{2}}$
Force on charge q due to the charge Q is $F_{2} = \frac{kQq}{r^{2}}$
In order to make the net force on q to be zero,
F1 = F2
$\frac{4kq^{2}}{r^{2}} = \frac{kQq}{r^{2}}$
$\Rightarrow Q = 4q$

Question7: Two point charges A and B, having charges +Q and –Q respectively, are placed at certain distance apart and force acting between them is F. If 25% charge of A is transferred to B, then force between the charges becomes

a) 4F/3

b) F

c) 9F/16

d) 16F/9

solution:

Force before transfer of charges
$F = \frac{kQ^{2}}{r^{2}}$
After the transfer of 25% charge of A to B then,
Charge at $A = Q - \frac{Q}{4} =\frac{3Q}{4}$
Charge at $B = -Q+\frac{Q}{4}=\frac{-3Q}{4}$
Force after the transfer of charges,
$F' = \frac{k(\frac{3Q}{4})(\frac{-3Q}{4})}{r^{2}}$ $=\frac{9kQ^{2}}{16r^{2}}= \frac{9F}{16}$
