Question2: Quantization of charge implies

a) Charge cannot be destroyed

b) Charge exists on particles

c) There is minimum permissible charge on a particle

d) All charges are integral multiple of basic unit of charge of an electron

Solution

Experimentally it is established that all free charges are integral multiples of a basic unit of charge denoted by e. Thus charge q on a body is always given by
q = ne
where n is any integer, positive or negative. This basic unit of charge is the charge that an electron or proton carries

Question6: Two point charges +4q and +q are placed 30 cm apart. Where should be a third charge kept on the line joining the two charges such that the third charge is at equilibrium?

a) 15 cm from the charge q

b) 7.5 cm from the charge q

c) 20 cm from the charge 4q

d) 5 cm from the charge q

Solution



The electric field will be zero at a point where the forces exerted by charges 4q and q on the third charge q0 are equal and opposite. Let this point be at a distance x from q and (30-x) from 4q.
If F1 is force due to 4q and F2 due to q then
F1 = F2
$\frac{1}{4\pi \epsilon_{0}} \frac{4qq_{0}}{\left ( 30-x \right )^{2}} = \frac{1}{4\pi \epsilon_{0}} \frac{qq_{0}}{x^{2}}$
Calculating, we get x = 10 cm and
(30 - x) =20 cm

Question7: Two charges q and -3q are placed on x axis separated by distance d. where should a third charge 2q be placed such that it will not experience any force

a) (d+√3d)/2

b) (d-√3d)/2

c) (d-3d)/2

d) (d+3d)/2

Solution

Since like charges attract repel each other while unlike charges attract each other, if the third charge is placed in between the two charges, the repulsive force due to the positive charge and the attractive force due to the negative charge will be in the same direction and therefore, the charge will not be in equilibrium. Therefore, we will place the charge at a distance x from the charge q in a direction away from the charge −3q as shown below.


Force on the charge 2q by the charge q is $\frac{k q 2q}{x^{2}}$
Force on the charge 2q by the charge 3q is $\frac{k \: 2q \: 3q}{\left ( d+x \right )^{2}}$
For equilibrium, F=0
$\frac{k q 2q}{x^{2}}$ = $\frac{k \: 2q \: 3q}{\left ( d+x \right )^{2}}$
$ x = \frac{d}{\left ( \sqrt{3}-1 \right )^{2}}$
Rationalizing we get
$x = \frac{d \left ( \sqrt{3}+1 \right )}{\left ( \sqrt{3}-1 \right ) \left ( \sqrt{3}+1 \right )} = \left ( \frac{d+\sqrt{3}d}{2} \right )$

Question8: If a charge q is placed at the centre of the line joining two equal charges Q such that the system is in equilibrium then the value of q is

a) –Q/4

b) Q/4

c) –Q/2

d) Q/2

Solution

At equilibrium, net force will be zero, hence $\frac{k q Q}{\frac{r}{2}^{2}}+\frac{kQ^{2}}{r^{2}}=0$
Calculating we get,
4q = -Q or q = -Q/4
