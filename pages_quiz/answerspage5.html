Question1: If an object is positively charged, theoretically the mass of the object ______

a) Increases slightly by a factor of $9.11\times 10^{-31}$ kg

b) Decreases slightly by a factor of $9.11\times10^{-31}$ kg

c) Remains the same (lost)

d) May increase or decrease

Explanation

If an object is positively charged it loses some of its electrons. The mass of an electron is $9.11\times10^{-31}$ kg. So, if a positively charged body loses ‘n’ number of electrons, it mass decrease by the amount
$n\times 9.11\times 10^{-31}kg.$

Question2: A man tried to hold a brass rod by hand and induce static electricity in it by friction. He failed to induce static electricity. Why did he fail?

a) Static electricity is induced only on insulators while brass is conducting

b) The induced static charge flew through his hand and body to the ground

c) The static charge was not produced at all

d) It is not possible to induce static electricity by friction

Explanation

The human body is a conductor of electricity and hence the charge produced by friction, flew through his body to the ground. We can tackle the problem simply by holding the brass rod with the help of some insulated holder.

Question3: Two equally charged spheres attract each other. Now if they touch each other then they will _____

a) Attract each other with the same amount of force

b) Repel each other

c) Attract each other with less amount of force

d) Don’t attract or repel

Explanation

The two spheres are oppositely charged by the same amount. So if they come in contact, both of their charges are neutralized. So they become uncharged and don’t attract or repel each other.

Question4: Which among the following cannot be the charge of a charged body?

a) $4.8 \times 10^{-14}$ Coulomb

b) $6.4 \times 10^{-15}$ Coulomb

c) $5 \times 10^{-19}$ Coulomb

d) $3.2 \times 10^{-10}$ Coulomb

Explanation

Charge of a body must be an integral multiple of charge of an electron(according to quantization of charges). $5 \times 10^{-14}$ is not an integral multiple of $1.602 \times 10^{-19} \; Coulomb$ (which is the charge of an electron). 4.8, 6.4, 3.2 are all integral multiples of 1.6.

Question5: A charge of magnitude q is divided into two parts such that force between resulting two charges is maximum when separated through some distance r. The division of charges would be

a) 3q/8 , 5q/8

b) 2q/4 , 2q/4

c) q/2 , q/2

d) 3q/6 , 3q/6

Solution

Suppose charge q is divided into two parts such that one part of charge is $q_{1}$ then second part is $(q−q_{1})$.
Therefore $F=\frac{1}{4\pi \epsilon_{0}}\; \frac{q_{1}\left (q-q_{1} \right )}{r^{2}}$
For F to be maximum $\frac{dF}{dq_{1}}=0$
$\frac{dF}{dq_{1}} = \frac{d}{dq_{1}}\left ( \frac{1}{4\pi \epsilon_{0}}\frac{\left ( qq_{1}- q_{1}^{2}\right )}{r^{2}} \right )$
$ =\frac{1}{4\pi \epsilon_{0}r^{2}}\left ( q-2q_{1} \right )=0$
$\therefore q_{1}=\frac{q}{2}$

Question10: Two identical metallic spheres of exactly equal masses are taken. One is given a positive charge of Q coulombs and the other is given an equal negative charge. Their masses after charging

a) Remain same

b) The sphere which is given positive charge weighs more

c) The sphere which is given negative charge weighs more

d) The sphere which is given negative charge weighs less

Explanation

A body is positively charged means it loses some of its electrons and if it is charged negatively implies that it gained some(n) electrons. So the mass of negatively charged body gains a weight by the amount $n\times9.11\times 10^{-31}kg$
